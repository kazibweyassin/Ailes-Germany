Getting Started

The Basics

What are the three main pathways to Germany?

+

1. Study: Attend a German university (tuition-free or low-cost in most states). Takes 3–4 years. Good if you want a degree and longer time to build German language skills.

2. Ausbildung: Vocational training for trades, IT, hospitality. You earn while you learn. Takes 2–3 years. Great if you want practical skills and immediate employment prospects.

3. Skilled Work: Get a job with visa sponsorship. Works for nurses, developers, care assistants, and specialists. Fastest pathway (4–8 months) if you have the right qualifications.

Which pathway is best for me?

+

It depends on your background:

— You have a university degree: Skilled Work (fastest) or Study for specialization

— You have no degree but vocational experience: Ausbildung to formalize skills

— You're a nurse or healthcare worker: Skilled Work (immediate demand)

— You're young (under 25) with secondary education: Ausbildung (earn + learn)

We assess your specific situation in a free consultation and give you honest guidance.

Is moving to Germany actually realistic for Africans?

+

Yes. Africa has a rapidly growing population of Germans in healthcare, IT, trades, and skilled work. Germany actively recruits from Africa for shortage occupations. We've helped dozens of professionals successfully move — from Uganda, Kenya, Nigeria, Ghana, South Africa, and beyond. It requires preparation and commitment, but it's achievable.

Language & Preparation

Getting Ready

Do I need to speak German before I apply?

+

It depends on the pathway:

— Ausbildung: B1–B2 German required. Start learning now.

— Nursing/Care Work: B1+ German required for visa approval

— Study: Some programs are English-taught, but B1 German helps with applications

— Skilled Work (IT/Dev): English often acceptable, but B1 German preferred for visa

We recommend reaching B1 before you apply. You can learn German online in 6–9 months of consistent study.

How long does it take to reach B1 German?

+

About 600–700 hours of study. In realistic terms: 6–9 months if you study 2–3 hours daily. Intensive courses can compress this to 3–4 months. Cost ranges €500–€2,500 depending on format (online, in-person, intensive).

What's the cost of German language training?

+

— Online courses: €500–€1,200 (Duolingo Plus, Babbel, Busuu)

— Classroom training: €800–€1,500 (local providers)

— Intensive programs: €1,500–€2,500 (compressed timeline)

— In Germany: Often included or subsidized by employers/training programs

Will my qualifications be recognized?

+

It depends on your field. Nursing and healthcare: Formal recognition required (1–6 months process, €200–€400). Trades/Ausbildung: Depends on apprenticeship requirements. IT/Development: Often no formal recognition needed if you prove competence. Other fields: Case-by-case assessment.

We help you assess this in your initial consultation — it's critical to know before you invest.

Visas & Legality

Official Pathways

Do employers really sponsor visas?

+

Yes. In our network, nurses, care workers, developers, and other skilled professionals all have employers covering visa costs. German employers actively recruit internationally. If you have the right skills and a solid job offer, visa sponsorship is common — not rare.

How much does a work visa cost?

+

The visa application fee is €100. However, employers often cover this as part of recruitment. Additional costs (health insurance, processing documents) are usually minimal. Most of your upfront cost goes to language training and travel, not visa fees.

How long does a work visa take to process?

+

Usually 4–8 weeks from job offer to approved visa. In some cases, you can start working while your visa is being processed. This varies by embassy and your home country. We help you manage this timeline.

What happens if my visa application is rejected?

+

Rejections are rare if you follow the process correctly. Common reasons for rejection: incomplete documents, unclear job offer, or mismatched qualifications. We help you prepare complete, strong applications. If rejected, you can appeal within 30 days. Most appeals succeed with stronger documentation.

Can I stay in Germany after my visa expires?

+

Yes. Work visas are renewable. After 3–5 years continuous employment, you can apply for permanent residence (Niederlassungserlaubnis). After 8 years legal residence, you can apply for German citizenship (if you speak B1 German and pass a civics test).

Money & Costs

Finances

How much does it cost to move to Germany?

+

Initial costs range €3,000–€7,500:

— German language course: €800–€2,500
— Language exam: €150–€250
— Work visa: €100 (often employer-covered)
— Flight: €600–€1,200
— Initial deposits & furnishing: €1,500–€2,500

The key: Most pathways pay salary while you work/train, so you recover costs within 3–6 months.

What's the cost of living in Germany?

+

Budget for monthly expenses:

— Rent (shared apartment): €400–€700
— Food & groceries: €200–€300
— Transport (monthly pass): €50–€100
— Phone, internet, utilities: €50–€100
— Personal/entertainment: €200–€300

Total: €900–€1,500/month (lower in smaller cities, higher in Berlin/Munich)

Healthcare and pension are mostly covered by your employer, so these aren't extra costs.

What salary can I expect?

+

— Nurse: €24,000–€32,000/year entry-level
— Ausbildung (during): €650–€1,200/month
— Ausbildung (after): €28,000–€35,000/year
— Care Assistant: €22,000–€26,000/year entry-level
— Developer: €35,000–€50,000/year depending on experience

Take-home is typically 60–70% of gross salary after taxes and social contributions.

Can I work part-time while studying or training?

+

Yes. Student visas allow 20 hours/week during semester + full-time during breaks. Ausbildung apprentices can work evenings/weekends (total max 20 hours/week outside training). Part-time work (€520/month) can help cover living costs.

Family & Life

Personal Considerations

Can my family come with me?

+

Yes. After you get a job and prove financial stability (usually 6–12 months of employment), your spouse and children can join via family reunification. Timeline: 2–3 months processing. Requirement: Proof of adequate income (usually €1,500+ net for family of 3) and housing.

How do I find housing in Germany?

+

Popular sites: ImmobilienScout24.de, Wunderground.de, Facebook housing groups
Budget: €400–€600/month for shared apartments in most cities
Employer support: Job offer letters often include housing assistance or temporary accommodations
Tip: Start searching 2–3 months before arrival. Many landlords require references or German bank account.

What's the quality of life like in Germany?

+

Germany ranks high globally for quality of life: excellent public transportation, free/affordable healthcare, strong worker protections, safe cities, good education systems. Social integration can take time (Germans are reserved but reliable). Most Africans adapt well within 6–12 months.
