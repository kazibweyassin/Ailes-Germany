What is Ausbildung?

Ausbildung is a unique German vocational training system. Unlike university, it's work-focused, employer-sponsored, and you earn salary from day one.

Key Facts

  • Duration: 3 years (sometimes 2–4 depending on the trade)
  • Format: 3–4 days per week at the company, 1–2 days at a vocational school (Berufsschule)
  • Salary: €650–€1,200/month (first year), increases each year
  • End result: Certified, recognized trade qualification (internationally respected)
  • Post-Ausbildung: Become an independent contractor, start a business, or work as a specialist

Who Can Do Ausbildung?

  • Education level: Secondary school diploma (not university required)
  • Age: Typically under 30 (but older applicants considered)
  • Language: German B1–B2 required (usually tested before hiring)
  • Visa status: You need a valid work visa to start

How Ausbildung Actually Works

Year 1: Foundation

  • You learn the basics from experienced trainers at the company
  • Vocational school teaches theory (materials, business principles, regulations)
  • Salary: €650–€850/month (you'll live modestly but can save money)
  • Focus: Building fundamental skills and understanding the trade

Year 2: Practice & Specialization

  • You take on real projects under supervision
  • You work with clients or in production alongside experienced staff
  • Salary increases: €750–€950/month
  • First intermediate exam (you must pass to continue)

Year 3: Mastery & Independence

  • You work almost independently on real projects
  • Employer may assign you complex tasks or client interaction
  • Salary increases: €850–€1,200/month
  • Final exam: Practical + theoretical (Prüfung)

Final Exam & Certification

  • Practical test: You demonstrate your skills (4–6 hours)
  • Written exam: Technical knowledge test
  • Oral exam: Discuss your work and understanding
  • Pass rate: ~70–80% (well-prepared apprentices almost always pass)
  • Certificate: Recognized in Germany, EU, and increasingly in Africa

Ausbildung Timeline

3 years from start to certification. Fast-track programs (2 years) available if you have relevant experience.

How to Get an Ausbildung Position

Ausbildung positions are competitive but attainable. The key is finding the right employer and demonstrating German language ability.

Step 1: Prepare Your German (3–9 months)

  • Most Ausbildung requires B1–B2 German level
  • Complete an online course or attend in-person classes
  • Cost: €500–€2,500
  • Timeline: 6–9 months of dedicated study (2–3 hours daily)

Step 2: Build Your Application (2–4 weeks)

  • German CV (Lebenslauf) highlighting relevant experience
  • Motivation letter (Anschreiben) explaining why you want this trade
  • Scans of your school certificates and any relevant qualifications
  • German language certificate (TestDaF or Goethe exam)

Step 3: Apply to Companies (2–4 months)

  • Apply directly to companies (best path)
  • Use job platforms: Indeed.de, StepStone.de, Azubiyo.de
  • Connect with recruitment agencies specializing in Ausbildung
  • We help coordinate with employers in our network

Step 4: Interview & Offer (2–6 weeks)

  • Phone/video interview (test your German and motivation)
  • In-person interview if progressing
  • Companies may request a probation period (paid)
  • Job offer usually comes with visa sponsorship promise

Step 5: Visa Application (4–8 weeks)

  • Once you have a job offer, apply for a work visa
  • German embassy processes your application
  • Cost: €100 (visa fee)
  • You can start once you have an approved job contract

Total Timeline: German Preparation to Ausbildung Start

9–16 months total. Most people take 12–14 months from first German lesson to starting their Ausbildung.

Career Growth Potential

  • Specialist/Senior Technician (3–5 years): €35,000–€45,000/year
  • Team Lead/Supervisor (5+ years): €45,000–€55,000/year
  • Self-Employed/Master's Level: €60,000–€100,000+/year

Why Earn While You Learn?

  • You're not going into debt for training (like university)
  • You can save money during apprenticeship for relocation, housing, family support
  • You finish with income-producing skills, not debt
  • German employers value Ausbildung certification — it's a credential that opens doors
