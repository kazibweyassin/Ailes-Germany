Work pathway

Move to Germany for skilled work

Germany actively recruits from Africa. New migration laws make it easier than ever for qualified professionals to secure work visas.

How the work visa works

  • Secure a job offer from a German employer
  • Employer begins sponsorship process
  • You apply for work visa with skill recognition documents
  • Visa typically issued within 4–8 weeks

What you need

  • Relevant qualifications or certifications
  • 1–3 years professional experience (typical)
  • Language skills (B1 German or job-specific English)
  • Valid passport and health insurance

Salary expectations

  • Entry-level roles: €20k–€28k/year
  • Mid-level (3–5 yrs): €28k–€45k/year
  • Senior roles: €45k–€70k+/year
  • Healthcare & IT typically pay above average

Timeline

  • Job search: 2–6 months
  • Visa sponsorship: 1–2 months
  • Visa processing: 4–8 weeks
  • Total: 4–9 months (typical)

Budget planning

What does it cost to move to Germany?

Transparent breakdown of typical visa, moving, and living costs so you can plan ahead.

Visa & application costs

  • Visa application fee: €80–€100
  • Qualification recognition (if needed): €100–€300
  • Medical examination: €50–€150
  • Document translation: €50–€200
  • Subtotal: €280–€750

Moving & setup (first month)

  • Flight from Uganda: €400–€700
  • Temporary accommodation (1–2 weeks): €300–€600
  • Registration/admin costs: €50–€100
  • Phone SIM & essentials: €30–€50
  • Subtotal: €780–€1,450

Monthly living costs (estimate)

  • Rent (shared apartment): €400–€600
  • Food & groceries: €200–€300
  • Transport pass: €50–€100
  • Utilities & internet: €100–€150
  • Monthly: €750–€1,150

Ailes Global consultation

  • Free eligibility check: no cost
  • Roadmap consultation: €50–€150 (one-time)
  • Full application support (optional): €300–€600
  • Ongoing WhatsApp guidance: included
  • Our typical cost: €0–€750

Total first-year estimate: €2,000–€4,500 (visa, moving, setup, and first 3 months living).
